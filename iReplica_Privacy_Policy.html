iReplica is a camera-based application. This app was developed by the jma team as a Free app. This app allows the user to capture photos in RAW and then process them in either the standard image processing pipline or our custom ONOFF processing pipeline. The primary purpose of this app is to be able to capture a photo, and, by processing the RAW, be able to replicate a very close representation of what the human eye sees. In short, make the photo look as realistic as possible. The app is currently in its initial stages, we will be improving the application regularly so stay tuned! Another purpose of this app is to provide image adjustment data for vision research in order to better understand the sense of sight. Since iReplica is a camera-based application, the main privacy points revolve around the captured image's content and location.

  • Camera, Photos, and Audio. Our service requires us to collect images and other information from your device’s camera and photos. For example, you won’t be able to capture or upload photos to your camera roll unless we can access your camera or photos. The application does NOT support Audio. Only the user has access to the captured image. We only have access to upload the photo to your Photos App, when you request so. We have no access to your content in your Photos. As soon as you close the application, or cancel the image, the image is forever deleted.

  • Location Information. When you use our services we will never collect information about your location.

  • Information Collected by Cookies, Log, and Other Technologies. We do NOT collect any information from cookies, logs, or any other technologies.

  • Device Information. We collect minimal non-personal information from and about the devices you use. The only information that we collect from the device is your screen brightness. The only information we collect about the device are: hardware model and minimal information on the devices sensor: (i.e. Aperture & Exposure). This collection of information is only done if the user consents to contributing to our research. More on this in the next paragraph.

  • Device Content. We collect minimal non-personal information on the your experience with the application. The only information we collect about the content are editing perferences such as your choice of gamma correction, contrast, alpha, and weight. This collection of information is only done if the user consents to contributing to our research. More on this in the next paragraph.

After you successfully save the photos in your Photos, you will be prompted with an option to contribute to our vision research. This contribution is done in the form of mailing a csv file, populated with data (more on what data is included down below), to our laboratory. In order to mail the data to us, you must have a valid email address connected to your Mail App. Once we receive the contribution, we will process the csv file and delete the email, removing any trace to your email address. The email address is not collected, stored, or shared in any way. In the Mail Composer, you will see the recipient as our laboratory email, if you would like to receive a copy of the csv datasheet, simply add your email address to the recipients. The email address will be retained to your device and we will have no access to it. We will simply receive a copy of your adjustment preferences, anonymously, for vision research or to develop future clinical applications. If you would like to restrain from sending an anonymous copy of your adjustment preferences to our laboratory, simply delete our email address in the Mail Composer, or refrain from contributing. The following data will be in the csv datasheet, none of which is linked to your identity:

  • A session ID which is nothing more than the applications name with a randomly generated number;
  • The processing method used when saving the photos;
  • Non-personal Device Information.
    • Screen brightness;
    • Lens Model (i.e. iPhone 8 back camera 3.99m f/1.8);
    • Information from the device's sensors: Brightness, Aperture, ISO, and Exposure;
  • Non-personal information on the Processing Used.
    • Black level, white level, and white balance;
    • Color conversion matrix;
    • Both of which are public information and hardware model specific;
  • Non-personal Information on the adjustment preferences.
    • The contrast, brightness, and gamma correction values used in the standard processing;
    • The alpha, beta, and weight values used in the ONOFF processing;
    • Your identity is not linked to these values, it is completely anonymous;
  • Histograms of captured photo's rgb pixel intensities.
    • Simply a list that is 255 items in length, for each color channel (red, green, blue);
    • Each item in the list represents the number of pixels across the image that had the same intensity as the item’s number in the order. (i.e. if the image has red and very bright, it would have 0’s in most of the list, and a couple of hundred thousands in the 240-255 range);
    • This is in NO way a representation of the image, nor is it possible to replicate the image captured from these counters;
